## Overview
This repository contains our submission for the Box Box Box F1 Simulator challenge. Our approach focused on entirely reverse-engineering the hidden physics model used to generate the historical race data, rather than relying on standard black-box machine learning. By building a mathematically pure, physical race engine, we were able to use simulated annealing to lock in the exact floating-point parameters governing tire degradation and track temperature interactions.

## 🔬 The Physics Engine (Race Simulator)
Our most significant breakthrough was discarding the flawed, additive baseline model and rebuilding the tire physics from the ground up. Our final engine relies on four core pillars:

1. **The Locked Cliffs:** We analyzed the degradation curves and identified that the "cliff" thresholds are absolute laws of the physics engine, not variable parameters. We locked these thresholds rigidly at exactly 10.0 (Soft), 20.0 (Medium), and 30.0 (Hard) laps.
2. **Base Pace & Degradation:** Post-cliff wear is calculated using a compound-specific formula combining a linear penalty and a quadratic penalty: `Wear = (Linear * Age) + (Quadratic * Age^2)`.
3. **The Multiplicative Temperature Discovery:** The baseline code erroneously applied track temperature as a flat, additive penalty to every lap. Through error analysis of our early models, we discovered that temperature is actually a **multiplicative factor** applied to the wear rate itself. Fresh tires are largely unaffected by extreme heat, while heavily worn tires degrade exponentially faster on a hot track.
4. **Deterministic Tie-Breaking:** To account for sub-millisecond photo finishes generated by floating-point math, we implemented a strict deterministic tie-breaker sorting by race time, then alphanumerically by Driver ID string.

## 🧠 The Optimizer (Calibration)
With a physically accurate simulator built, we deployed a custom optimization script to find the exact compound variables.

* **Algorithm:** Simulated Annealing. We designed custom mutation constraints that prevented the AI from testing physically impossible scenarios (e.g., tires getting faster as they age, or negative quadratic bounds).
* **Gradient-Guided Exact Match:** The baseline calibrator optimized for Spearman's rank correlation. However, because the competition scores primarily on exact 20/20 matches, we overhauled the objective function. We rewarded `1.0` points exclusively for a perfect array match, combined with a micro-gradient (`0.01 * pairwise correlation`) to ensure the simulated annealing algorithm still had a "slope" to climb without settling for "close enough."

## 🐛 Key Learnings & Error Analysis
During training, our initial models hit a "perfect" score on the training set but failed catastrophically on the test set. Upon investigation, we realized our early additive temperature model allowed the optimizer to find a mathematical loophole—it inverted the base pace of the tires (making Hards the fastest) and aggressively tweaked the flat temperature penalty to compensate, perfectly overfitting the 800 training races. 

By rewriting the engine to use a multiplicative `wear_loss = base_degradation * temp_factor`, we closed this loophole, forcing the optimizer to solve the actual physical puzzle and resulting in highly generalizable weights.
